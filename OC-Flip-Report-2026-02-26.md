## Part 5: Market Trends Affecting OC Flips (Feb 2026)

### Rate Environment
- 30-year fixed still hovering 6.5‚Äì7.0% range. Not ideal for buyer pool, but OC's high-cash buyer concentration (~25‚Äì30% cash sales historically) means flip exits are still viable. Buyers who can pay cash or 20%+ down are less rate-sensitive.

### Buyer Demand
- OC-wide sales volume down 24.8% MoM (Jan 2026, CAR) ‚Äî partly seasonal (Jan is always slow). The zip-level data tells a different story: Garden Grove 92843 at 102.4% sale-to-list and Westminster 92683 at 100.1% with 31 DOM suggest pent-up demand in mid-tier price ranges.
- Renovated, turnkey SFRs under $1.1M remain scarce. This is your product. The buyer segment (move-up buyers, growing families) is rate-resistant enough to transact.

### Distressed Inventory
- CA foreclosure filings remain below 2019 levels but pre-foreclosure activity is ticking up statewide. OC estate/probate inventory is the most reliable distressed source ‚Äî aging demographic in cities like Fullerton (92832), Santa Ana, and Anaheim means consistent turnover.
- Probate inventory in OC: Best accessed via court-filed probate petitions (Orange County Superior Court, Probate Division), not MLS. Many probate properties are sold off-market or through real estate attorneys.

### Permit Timelines
- City of Anaheim: 4‚Äì8 weeks for kitchen/bath permits; major additions 4‚Äì6 months.
- City of Santa Ana: 6‚Äì10 weeks for standard renos; historically slower than Anaheim.
- Garden Grove: 4‚Äì6 weeks for cosmetic permits.
- **Implication:** Light-to-medium rehabs (cosmetic + permitted mechanical) should target 3‚Äì4 month hold. Budget 6 months for heavy rehabs in Santa Ana.

### Market Softening (OC High End)
- OC median SFH declined 1.4% YoY ‚Äî but this is skewed by the luxury end (Newport Beach, Laguna Beach). The $800K‚Äì$1.1M mid-market segment is *still appreciating* (+7.7% in Stanton, +8.6% in Garden Grove, +11.7% in Westminster). This is precisely the flip exit price range. Trend is your friend.
